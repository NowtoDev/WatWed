Best temples to visit

in Chiang Mai

Come and experience the beauty of the temples in Chiang Mai, where history, stunning artistry, and sacredness are woven into every corner. Discover the rich stories, admire the exquisite craftsmanship, and feel the spiritual essence that permeates each temple.

Discover the 10 most stunning temples in Chiang Mai and immerse yourself in spiritual experiences.

1.Wat Sri Suphan

Wat Sri Suphan Located south of the moat on Wualai Road, this is another ancient temple in Chiang Mai, built in 2043 by King Philokpanadda Thirat or Phra Mueang Kaew, the King of Chiang Mai. Inside the temple is the Silver Ubosot, which was renovated from the base and boundary of the original Ubosot that was damaged, making this the first Silver Ubosot in Thailand. Inside the Ubosot is the principal Buddha image, Phra Chao Chet Tue, in the Mara-Vijaya attitude. There is also a Lanna-style chapel that has been renovated and combined with contemporary art, while preserving the original features. There are also mural paintings depicting the 12 zodiac signs, images of the pagoda, the center of Chiang Mai in the past, and riddles for tourists to see.

Address: Wualai Road, Hai Ya Subdistrict, Mueang District, Chiang Mai Province
Coordinates : https://goo.gl/maps/9t2Ao8LczQEaUmeq8

2.Wat PhraThat DoiSuthep

Wat PhraThat DoiSuthep Located on the top of Doi Suthep, it was built in the early 19th Buddhist century during the reign of King Kue Na Thammarat, the 6th ruler of Chiang Mai, to enshrine the relics of the Lord Buddha that he had invited from Si Satchanalai. Wat Phra That Doi Suthep is considered a second-class royal temple of the Ratchaworawihan type and has been a sacred place of Chiang Mai since ancient times. If anyone visits Chiang Mai and does not go up to pay homage, it is considered as if they have not arrived in Chiang Mai. Wat Phra That Doi Suthep has a seven-headed naga staircase made of cement, approximately 300 steps, or you can use the cable car to go up to pay homage to the chedi. Inside the temple, there is a Chiang Saen-style pagoda with a high base and recessed bell-shaped corners, octagonal, covered with two layers of gold leaf, which was built in the Lanna art style. From the top, you can clearly see the view of Chiang Mai.

Address: 9 Village No. 9, Mueang District, Chiang Mai Province
Coordinates : https://goo.gl/maps/CVC2eESQN6dyfxPn8

3.Wat Phra Singh Worawihan

Wat Phra Singh Worawihan It is an important temple in the history of the Lanna Kingdom since the past. It is a first-class royal temple, Wora Maha Wihan type, located in the moat of Chiang Mai, Sam Lan Road, Phra Singh Subdistrict, Mueang District. Wat Phra Singh Worawihan is one of the important temples of Chiang Mai. Inside is Phra Singh or Phra Phuttha Sihing, which is an ancient Buddha image in the ancient Chiang Saen art style, in the Mara-Vijaya posture, sitting cross-legged in the diamond meditation posture. It was built in 700 B.E. and is known as "Chiang Saen Singh Neung". It is also a sacred Buddha image of Chiang Mai and the Lanna Kingdom. Inside Wat Phra Singh Worawihan, there is also Wihan Lai Kham, which enshrines Phra Phuttha Sihing. There are also mural paintings of Suphannahong and Sangthong, which can only be found here. There are also other interesting works of art, such as the ordination hall decorated in the Lanna art style, the library decorated with stucco angels, and a round Lanna-style pagoda. In addition, according to beliefs, the pagoda of Wat Phra Singh is considered the pagoda of the birth year of those born in the year of the dragon (large snake). If you come to pay homage at least once, it will be the most auspicious, giving you a long life and prosperity forever.

Address: 2 Samlan Road, Phra Singh Subdistrict, Mueang District, Chiang Mai Province
Coordinates : https://goo.gl/maps/RrAtKhRsrm3fD5jDA

4.Wat Chedi Luang

Wat Chedi Luang It is an old royal temple of Chiang Mai, located in the center of the city on Phra Pok Klao Road. It has a spacious area. When you walk in, you will find the Lanna-style royal chapel in the middle. Chao Khun Ubali, the grand master, and Chao Kaew Nawarat built it in 2471. Inside, there is the principal Buddha image, Phra Attharot, in the attitude of presiding over an apology, according to early Chiang Mai art influenced by Pala art (India). In front of the entrance to the chapel, there is a staircase of Naga slithering with their tails intertwined to form the archway of the chapel, which looks very beautiful. This pair of Naga is an ancient work of art that has been around since the beginning and is considered the most beautiful Naga in the North. Wat Chedi Luang Wihan is the place where the largest pagoda in Chiang Mai is enshrined. It was built during the reign of King Saen Mueang Mak, the seventh king of the Mangrai Dynasty. Later, Phraya Tilokarat ordered craftsmen to expand the pagoda to be higher and wider than before and invited the Emerald Buddha to be enshrined for 80 years. Later, in 2088 during the reign of Queen Jiraprapa, an earthquake occurred, causing the top of the pagoda to collapse.

Address: 103 Phra Pok Klao Road, Sriphum Subdistrict, Mueang District, Chiang Mai Province
Coordinates : https://goo.gl/maps/ycbPaFgRjyKefuTFA

5.Wat Phan Tao

Wat Phan Tao An ancient temple in the heart of the city, located on Phra Pok Klao Road, Phra Singh Subdistrict, Mueang District. It was originally a sanctuary and a place for casting the Attharos Buddha image of Wat Chedi Luang. Later, it was built as Wat Phan Tao. In the past, Chiang Mai people called this place “Pan Tao” (Thousand Times) temple, which means that if you make merit just once, you will receive merit in return a thousand times. Later, it was distorted to “Phan Tao”. Another reason is probably because this temple was used as a source for building hundreds of kilns to cast Buddha images, so it was called “Wat Phan Tao”. Inside this temple, there is the most beautiful and complete Vihara in Chiang Mai Province. The building is made entirely of teakwood, in the Chiang Saen style. It was originally a Ho Kham or Khum Luang residence of Phra Chao Mahotarades, the 5th ruler of Chiang Mai. During the reign of Phra Chao Inthawichanon (the 7th ruler), he ordered the demolition of this Ho Kham and offered it to be the Vihara of Wat Phan Tao. There is also an ancient-style raised pulpit that is over a hundred years old for visitors to see. Behind the Vihara Ho Kham Luang is the principal chedi of the temple, shaped like a bell on an octagonal base, surrounded by beautiful chedis.

Address: 127/7 Phra Pok Klao Road, Sriphum Subdistrict, Mueang District, Chiang Mai Province
Coordinates : https://goo.gl/maps/B57tLS6G8u1rZe1a8

6.Chiang Man Temple

Chiang Man Temple Located on Ratchaphakinai Road, Mueang District, this is the oldest temple in Chiang Mai city. It has been around since the city was first established. In 1839, King Mangrai donated Chiang Man Palace to be a monastery and named Wat Chiang Man. He also ordered the construction of the Elephant Surrounded Chedi in the area of ​​his residence. There are two wihans in Wat Chiang Man: the large wihan built in the present era. Between the windows of this wihan are beautiful gold-painted murals on a red background, telling the story of King Mangrai's establishment of the city and temples, both Wiang Kum Kam and Chiang Mai. The small wihan or Chaturamuk wihan is the original wihan of the temple. Inside is the enshrined important Buddha images of Chiang Mai. The ordination hall is very important to the people of Chiang Mai because in the front hall there is a stone inscription of Wat Chiang Man, which records the story of the establishment of Chiang Mai and the history of this temple, as well as the maintenance of the temple by the royal family. In addition, Wat Chiang Man also has the Elephant Surrounded Chedi as the main wihan, built in the Lanna style. There is also a library in the middle of the water to store the Tripitaka and other scriptures according to the principles of construction and wisdom of Lanna.

Address: 171 Ratchaphakin Road, Sriphum Subdistrict, Mueang District, Chiang Mai Province
Coordinates : https://goo.gl/maps/2Yp9GYteKn6QXENRA

7.Umong Temple

Umong Temple Located on Suthep Road, Mueang District, it was built during the reign of King Mangrai around 1839 for the forest dwellers to reside. Later, King Kuena built a tunnel for Phra Mahathera Chan to use as a place for meditation. This tunnel has a wall inside with many passageways. Inside the tunnel, there used to be murals, which are assumed to have been painted between 1900-2000 B.E. Originally, the murals covered the entire area of ​​every room. Most of them were pictures of lotus flowers, peonies, and various birds such as peacocks, herons, parrots, and ducks. On top of the tunnel is an old Lanna pagoda. Archaeologists assume that it was built around the beginning of the 20th Buddhist century. It is a bell-shaped pagoda with about 3 round tiers like stacked lotus petals. On top is a spire. In front of the tunnel is a carved stone Buddha head from the Phayao style, 1950-2100 B.E. The temple area is a Buddhist garden, shady with various kinds of trees. It is a forest garden suitable for meditation. Behind is a forest garden and zoo, which is a sanctuary where you can walk around and is another good place for bird watching.

Address: 135 Village No. 10, Muang District, Chiang Mai Province
Coordinates : https://goo.gl/maps/d2B6GQNDUFDArVRYA

8.Suan Dok Temple

Suan Dok Temple Located on Suthep Road, Suthep Subdistrict, Mueang District, in the past, it was a flower garden of the northern princes of the Mangrai Dynasty. In 1914, King Kuena, the 6th king of the Mangrai Dynasty, graciously ordered the construction of a “royal temple” to be the residence of “Phra Maha Thera Suman”, who established the Sri Lankan Buddhist sect in the Lanna region, and built a pagoda to enshrine the Buddha’s relics. Inside Wat Suan Dok, there are many ancient and beautiful religious sites and objects, such as the large Sri Lankan-style pagoda, which is a large pagoda enshrining the Buddha’s relics; Phra Chao Kao Tue, which is a large cast Buddha image made of 9 crore tamlung metal, or approximately 9 thousand chang; Phra Phuttha Patima Ka King, which is the principal Buddha image in the royal chapel; and a Lanna-style sermon pulpit. In addition, Wat Suan Dok also has annual traditions, which are held every year, such as the merit-making ceremony of the Salak Phat offering. Coincides with the 8th waxing moon of the 11th lunar month, the tradition of setting up a royal sermon or listening to the Mahachat sermon after the end of Buddhist Lent every year, and the tradition of making merit by bathing the Phra Borommathat, coincides with the 15th waxing moon of the 3rd lunar month every year.

Address: 139 Suthep Road, Suthep Subdistrict, Mueang District, Chiang Mai Province
Coordinates : https://goo.gl/maps/FSoL2Wxyh2AsA6Zt9

9.Wat Lok Moli

Wat Lok Moli Located on Maninoparat Road, Sriphum Subdistrict, it was built in 2070 by Phra Kesaklao who granted Ban Hua Wiang to be a temple. After that, a pagoda and a temple were built. When Phra Kesaklao passed away, his ashes were placed in the pagoda of this temple. Wat Lok Moli became a deserted temple for hundreds of years. Later, in 2544, the Chiang Mai Sangha restored it from being a deserted temple to a temple with monks residing during the Buddhist Lent. They developed and restored Wat Lok Moli to be a temple with monks residing during the Buddhist Lent in accordance with the law. The religious buildings inside Wat Lok Moli are exquisitely beautiful. There is a castle-shaped pagoda with sculptures of angels decorating the corners of the pagoda. The Royal Vihara is a newly built vihara after the temple was renovated. It is in the Lanna art style. Inside, there is a Buddha statue in meditation called “Phra Phuttha Santi Chirabrom Lokanath”. The ceiling and pillars are also beautifully carved. The Queen Chiraprabha Mahadevi Pavilion inside enshrines a cast statue of Queen Chiraprabha Mahadevi. and the monks' quarters and the Somdej's quarters, which were built with a mixture of Lanna-style sculptures

Address: 298/1 Maninoprat Road, Sriphum Subdistrict, Mueang District, Chiang Mai Province
Coordinates : https://goo.gl/maps/ojNmgtHbHpcbjWYq5

10.Wat Chedi Yot

Wat Chedi Yot Located in Chang Phueak Subdistrict on the Superhighway (Chiang Mai-Lampang), this is an important and ancient temple in Chiang Mai. It is similar to the Mahabodhi Temple in Bodh Gaya in India. The base of the pagoda is decorated with stucco figures of deities. The outside of the pagoda is decorated with stucco figures of deities, both sitting and standing, wearing different patterns, which are beautiful and worth seeing. Stupa of King Tilokarat When King Tilokarat passed away in 2030, Phra Yot Chiang Rai, his grandson, succeeded to the throne and ordered the construction of a large stupa to contain the ashes of his grandfather, which later became Satta Maha Sathan, which are seven important places in the life of the Buddha: Bodhi Ballang, Animit Chedi, Ratanajongkrom Chedi, Ratanaghara Chedi, Ajapalanigrod Chedi, and Rajatana Chedi. Currently, only three remain at Wat Chet Yot: Animit Chedi, Ratanaghara Chedi, and Mucalinda Chedi. It is also the temple where the 8th world Tripitaka recitation was held, the first in Thailand, and is considered the main practice of monks in Lanna. Wat Chet Yot is therefore a temple of great historical significance. In addition, the architecture inside the temple is magnificent and beautiful, worthy of respect and visiting.

Address: 90 Village No., Superhighway Road, Chiang Mai-Lampang, Mueang District, Chiang Mai Province
Coordinates : https://goo.gl/maps/1HQeFJT8TSRCuZxE6
